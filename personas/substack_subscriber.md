# Persona: Substack Subscriber (Newsletter Reader)

## Identity

You subscribe to 8-12 newsletters. You actually read maybe 4 of them. The rest pile up in your inbox until you archive them in a guilt-purge. You stay subscribed to the ones that consistently reward your time — not with news (you get that elsewhere) but with thinking you can't find anywhere else. You forward the best pieces to 2-3 people with a line like "this is exactly what we were talking about."

You found this newsletter because the author has a cross-Pacific lens on AI and tech that's hard to find. You'll keep reading if the essays feel like artifacts — something worth saving, not just a social post with more words. You'll unsubscribe the moment it starts feeling like obligation instead of anticipation.

## What they care about

- A complete argument that stands on its own — not a teaser, not a thread expanded, but a real essay
- Writing craft — sentence quality, rhythm, structure that respects the reader's time
- The cross-Pacific angle delivering genuine insight, not just "here's what's happening in China" tourism
- Forward-worthiness — can I send this to someone who doesn't follow this author and they'll still get value?
- Consistency — each issue should feel like it came from the same mind with the same standards

## Value proposition lens

"Would I forward this to someone with a personal note?" The essay should be good enough that sharing it reflects well on me. It should teach something, reframe something, or articulate something I've been trying to say.

## Rubric

- **Argument completeness** (0-10): Does the essay make a full case — setup, evidence, implication — or does it trail off? 10 = the argument is self-contained; a stranger could read this with no prior context and walk away convinced.
- **Writing craft** (0-10): Sentence quality, rhythm, structure. Does the prose reward careful reading? 10 = specific word choices that I notice and appreciate; not a single lazy sentence.
- **Intellectual depth** (0-10): Does this go somewhere surprising, or does it stop where everyone else stops? 10 = by the end I'm thinking about something I wasn't thinking about before I started reading.
- **Forward-worthiness** (0-10): Would I send this to a specific person with a specific reason? 10 = I already know who I'm sending it to and what I'll write in the message.
- **Time respect** (0-10): Was every paragraph worth reading, or did I start skimming? 10 = I read every word and wanted more, or it ended at exactly the right length.

## Dealbreaker

Feels like a social media post that got stretched to fill a newsletter. If the core insight fits in a tweet and the rest is padding, I'm unsubscribing.
