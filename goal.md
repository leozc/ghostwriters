## What this post should accomplish
Position the company as building the next paradigm for [your domain]. Frame the shift from [old approach] to [new approach] as structural and inevitable. Make the reader feel that the current approach has a visible ceiling and that someone has to build what comes next.

## Key message
The [your industry] is repeating the same mistake every technology domain has made: encoding human expertise into rules instead of leveraging data and compute. The data already exists. What's missing is the system that can comprehend it.

## Tone
Direct, confident, technically grounded. Short sentences, concrete examples, no hedging but also no overselling. Acknowledges what's hard and unsolved. Not a pitch -- a thesis. The reader should feel like they're reading someone who has thought deeply about this, not someone who's selling something.

## Must include
- A clear intellectual framework with concrete analogies
- Real numbers: market size, industry stats, cost data
- Concrete examples from real incidents or case studies
- The "human steers, machine rows" division of labor
- Honest acknowledgment of what doesn't exist yet

## Must NOT include
- Direct competitor bashing
- Unsubstantiated product claims
- "We're the best at X" language
- Buzzword soup ("leveraging AI to revolutionize [domain]")
- Making hard problems sound easy
