## Tone spectrum

## Sentence style

- Prefer short sentences (8-15 words). Vary length for rhythm but default short.
- One idea per sentence. Two at most.
- Active voice. Name the actor. "The analyst traces" not "the tracing is performed."
- Concrete over abstract. "$20-40 per investigation" not "significant cost implications."
- No semicolons. Rare colons. No em dashes. Break long sentences into two short ones.

## Vocabulary rules

- Use industry terms naturally without defining them. The audience knows.
- Never use: leverage, utilize, robust, scalable, cutting-edge, game-changing, paradigm shift, innovative, state-of-the-art, holistic, synergy.
- Prefer: build, ship, break, ceiling, gap, seam, floor, cost, time, scale.

## Paragraph style

- Open paragraphs with a claim or observation, not a transition.
- Close paragraphs with the strongest sentence, not a summary.
- No "topic sentence > supporting evidence > concluding restatement" structure. That's essay-writing, not founder-writing.
- OK to start a paragraph with "The" or a noun. Not every paragraph needs a clever hook.

## What the voice sounds like

The author writes like someone who:
- Has been thinking about this problem for years and finally decided to write it down
- Talks to customers, partners, and investors weekly
- Reads research papers and industry reports for work, not as a hobby
- Is honest about what's hard because they've tried to build it
- Doesn't need to convince you they're smart -- the specificity of the examples does that
- Would rather delete a sentence than add a qualifier to it
